The experiment is analysis-only. It does not regenerate article-text classifications.

4) What a supervision-quality row looks like

Experiment 008 introduces a single computed row per article. The score is not a learned confidence model; it is a transparent rule-based gate for downstream supervision.

Scoring rule

quality_score =
  relevance_score
  + spatial_score
  + evidence_density_score
  + article_type_score
  - uncertainty_penalty

quality_low:       score < 3
quality_medium:    3 <= score < 5
quality_high:      5 <= score < 7
quality_very_high: score >= 7

Example row

{
  "title": "Altenweiher",

5) Recommended-use precedence

The final bucket is intentionally conservative. Exclusion overrides otherwise strong spatial evidence when relevance is absent, uncertainty is high, or the final quality score is low.

PrecedenceRuleInterpretation
1exclude if quality low, relevance none, or uncertainty highKeep noisy rows out of downstream supervision.
2use_for_evaluation_only if very high quality, spatial 250 m >= 0.9, and high relevanceStrict diagnostic subset with strong point-label support.
3use_for_training if high/very high quality, spatial 250 m >= 0.8, and medium/high relevanceCandidate weak labels for later model training.
4inspect_manually otherwise, especially medium quality, unclear type, or medium uncertaintyUseful but not trusted automatically.

Source: evaluate_supervision_quality_score.py.

11) Implementation notes

  • Evaluator: evaluate_supervision_quality_score.py.
  • The experiment reads frozen Qwen and Gemma prediction directories, so reruns are fast and deterministic.
  • Rows are joined by string pageid to avoid numeric id drift across CSV/JSON inputs.
  • Outputs include both full scored rows and the candidate subset used by later weak-label/vision experiments.
  • The score intentionally penalizes high uncertainty and gives no article-type bonus to other_or_unclear.

12) Interpretation and next step

  • Use quality scores for data selection: they provide a compact, auditable way to rank candidate training and evaluation rows.
  • Keep relevance + spatial as the diagnostic baseline: it remains simpler and often stronger.
  • Manual inspection still matters: the largest bucket is inspect_manually, not an automatic use bucket.
  • Downstream experiments can reuse the rows: experiments 009-012 consume these scores for evidence cards, highlights, retrieved windows, and CLIP weak labels.

Recommended next step: use use_for_training and use_for_evaluation_only as conservative candidate pools, while reporting relevance + spatial as the primary comparison baseline.
