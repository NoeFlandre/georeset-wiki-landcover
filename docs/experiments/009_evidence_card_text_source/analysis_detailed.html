Experiment 009: Evidence-Card Text Source

Detailed implementation and result audit for deterministic evidence-card inputs, compared with raw content, summaries, and earlier land-use summaries.

1) Executive summary

Qwen rerun

Experiment 009 tests whether a deterministic, no-place evidence card can help Qwen classify land cover better than the previous short land-use evidence summary. The card is built from existing metadata; no LLM is used to create it.

11) Implementation notes

  • Card builder: evidence_cards.py.
  • Card generation CLI: build_evidence_cards.py.
  • Comparison evaluator: evaluate_evidence_card_experiment.py.
  • The standalone card uses existing metadata only: relevance, evidence sentences, article type, spatial confidence, and quality score.
  • content_with_evidence_card prepends the same card to the raw article, so it is not a no-place source.

12) Interpretation and next step

  • Evidence cards are better compact evidence than experiment 5 summaries: CORINE balanced accuracy rises from 0.184 to 0.224.
  • They are not a replacement for raw content: raw content keeps better balanced CORINE behavior and stronger OSM metrics.
  • The combined source guides majority-like decisions: CORINE accuracy improves, but macro-F1 drops.
  • The next text-only direction is highlighting or retrieval: keep raw content, but focus attention on evidence sentences instead of replacing the article with a compact card.

Recommended next step: use evidence cards as structured metadata and diagnostics, while testing highlighted-content or retrieved-window inputs that preserve raw article context.
