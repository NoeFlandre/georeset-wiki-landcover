Experiment 012: CLIP Linear Probe Weak Labels

Detailed implementation and result audit for training a simple image classifier from GeoReset weak CORINE labels.

1) Executive summary

First image experiment

Experiment 012 moves from text-only diagnostics to satellite-image classification. It fetches Sentinel-2 RGB patches at article coordinates, embeds them with frozen CLIP image features, and trains a small NumPy linear probe to predict CORINE level-2 labels.

9) Zero-shot comparison

13) Interpretation

  • Weak labels are useful: the best linear probe triples zero-shot accuracy on the strict eval split.
  • Hard quality filtering is too costly here: strict tiers remove rare-class examples that the probe needs.
  • Spatial-only is a strong compromise: it keeps fewer examples than all while nearly matching or beating macro-F1.
  • This is a data-policy test: frozen CLIP plus a linear head is intentionally simple and not the ceiling for Sentinel classification.

The result does not say quality and agreement signals are useless. It says they should be soft weights, sampling controls, calibration signals, or evaluation filters rather than hard training filters at this scale.

14) Next step

Do not train only on text_spatial_agreement. Keep broad training coverage, use quality signals softly, and reserve stricter rows for evaluation or calibration.

Recommended next experiment: use a broader weak-label training set with quality-aware sampling or loss weights, ideally with a Sentinel-native encoder rather than generic RGB CLIP.
