GeoReSeT experiment review

What the experiments show so far

We are testing whether French Wikipedia text and geolocated article coordinates can produce useful land-cover supervision. The core result: article text contains real signal, but label reliability, relevance, class imbalance, and image-model data coverage matter more than another compact text rewrite. Randomization controls now confirm that the strongest CORINE subset gains are not just support-size or class-mix artifacts.

Research Arc

Quick Decoder

The labels below define the project shorthand used in the reports and tables. They are intentionally compact so the experiment logic is readable at a glance.

Task

Predict land-cover labels for geolocated Wikipedia articles. CORINE is single-label European land cover; OSM is multi-label OpenStreetMap land-use tags.

Full article text

The complete extracted Wikipedia article body used as classifier input. Older tables call this content; this page names it explicitly.

Summary / no-place summary

LLM-compressed article text. The no-place variant removes obvious place names to reduce memorization from names rather than land-cover evidence.

Aligned vs shuffled text

Aligned means the article receives its own text. Shuffled means it receives another article's text; this is the control for text signal.

250 m spatial purity

Share of CORINE pixels/area in a 250 m buffer around the article coordinate that match the point label. A threshold >=0.8 marks cleaner labels.

Land-cover relevance

An LLM-generated evidence label from article text: none/low/medium/high depending on how much explicit forest, agriculture, water, urban, etc. evidence appears. Later filters use this label deterministically.

Evidence card

A compact, deterministic no-place fact sheet assembled from metadata: relevance, article type, spatial confidence, and land-cover evidence snippets.

Highlights / windows

Highlights prepend matched evidence sentences to the article. Retrieved windows keep raw neighboring sentences around evidence matches; random windows are the control.

Quality score

A deterministic supervision score combining relevance, spatial purity, evidence density, article type, and uncertainty penalties.

Text agreement tier

For CLIP training, this stricter tier keeps examples where Qwen, Gemma, and spatial labels agree. It is cleaner but smaller.

Multiscale image probe

Experiment 14 fetches Sentinel-2 RGB crops at known physical widths, then resizes each crop to 224x224 before CLIP embedding.

Supported metric

Supported balanced accuracy and macro-F1 average only over classes that actually appear in the evaluation split. This is the Experiment 14 headline metric.

Balanced accuracy

Average recall over classes. It is the main CORINE metric because majority classes can make raw accuracy look better than minority-class behavior.

OSM exact / Jaccard

OSM can have multiple labels. Exact match requires the whole label set; Jaccard gives partial credit for overlapping predicted and target tags.

Random subset controls

Experiment 13 compares each observed filter to random subsets with the same size, then to random subsets with the same target distribution.

At A Glance

1. Text is informative

Aligned article text beats shuffled controls. For Qwen CORINE full article text, balanced accuracy is 0.281 versus 0.067 shuffled.

2. Ground truth quality matters

Only 646 of 1,251 articles pass point-label share >= 0.8 at 250 m. On that cleaner subset, Qwen full-article-text balanced accuracy rises to 0.352.

3. Relevance is the best filter

Medium/high land-cover relevance plus 250 m spatial purity gives the strongest text diagnostic: Qwen full-article-text balanced accuracy 0.409.

4. Compact text is lossy

Evidence cards, highlights, and retrieved windows preserve signal, but none clearly replaces the complete Wikipedia article body for the main benchmark.

5. Models trade off differently

Gemma improves raw CORINE accuracy and OSM exact/micro metrics, while Qwen is stronger on balanced CORINE behavior.

6. Image probing is unresolved

Experiment 012 was promising: CLIP linear probing reached 0.600 accuracy. After fixing a Sentinel CRS bug, Experiment 014's multiscale MVP reaches 0.686.

7. CORINE filters survive randomization

Qwen relevance+spatial full-article-text CORINE balanced accuracy is 0.409 versus a 0.281 target-matched random mean.

Current Research Position

Best concise interpretation: Wikipedia text is useful for land-cover supervision when articles are relevant and point labels are spatially reliable. The best text input remains the complete Wikipedia article body. Deterministic summaries, cards, highlights, and retrieved windows are useful for diagnostics, filtering, and cost control, but not as primary replacements. For image learning, the original broad weak-label CLIP probe was promising, and the corrected multiscale CLIP-base MVP now strengthens that path: broad training reaches 0.686 strict supported balanced accuracy, while zero-shot CLIP remains at 0.200. Soft weighting is promising but not universally better than all-unweighted training. Random subset controls strengthen the CORINE claim, while OSM remains more sensitive to target composition and small support.
