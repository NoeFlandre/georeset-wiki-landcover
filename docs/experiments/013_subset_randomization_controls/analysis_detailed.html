Experiment 013: Subset Randomization Controls

Detailed implementation and result audit for Monte Carlo controls on filtered article subsets.

1) Executive summary

Analysis-only control experiment

Experiment 013 tests whether the filtered-subset gains from earlier article-text experiments survive random baselines. It reuses frozen predictions and metadata; it does not rerun LLMs, prompts, labels, spatial confidence, training, or GPU jobs.

3) Processing flow

flowchart TD A["frozen Qwen + Gemma predictions"] --> B["observed filtered subsets"] C["spatial, relevance, quality, use metadata"] --> B B --> D["compute observed CORINE BA or OSM Jaccard"] B --> E["sample random_same_n controls"] B --> F["sample random_same_target_distribution controls"] E --> G["compare observed score to random distribution"] F --> G G --> H["significant_filter_summary.csv"]

Each control samples from the same parent experiment, model, task, text source, and required metadata universe as the observed subset.

4) What a randomization-control row means

Experiment 013 introduces two controls. random_same_n asks whether a filter beats a random subset of the same size. random_same_target_distribution asks whether it still beats random subsets after preserving CORINE target counts, or OSM target-label-set counts.

5) Control definitions

ControlWhat is held fixedWhat it tests
random_same_nParent experiment, model, task, text source, metadata availability, and subset size.Whether the observed filter is better than drawing the same number of eligible rows at random.
random_same_target_distributionEverything in same-n plus CORINE target counts or OSM target-label-set counts.Whether the gain survives after controlling for target composition.
unstable_small_n=trueRows with fewer than 30 examples.Marks results that are useful diagnostics but too small for strong claims.

Spatial subsets are compared only against rows with the relevant spatial metadata. That avoids giving the random baseline rows that could never have passed the observed filter.

10) Interpretation

  • CORINE claim strengthened: geolocated Wikipedia text is most predictive when article relevance and spatial label reliability are credible, and that is not explained by support size or target class composition.
  • Spatial confidence remains useful: the 250 m purity filter survives target-matched controls for raw-content CORINE.
  • Quality plus spatial is a proxy: it behaves like a strong compact selection rule, but does not beat the best relevance plus spatial row.
  • OSM claim softened: some Qwen content subsets survive, but target composition and small support explain more of the gain than for CORINE.
  • Evaluation-only rows are diagnostics: rows marked unstable_small_n=true should not be treated as final evidence.

11) Implementation notes

  • Evaluator: evaluate_subset_randomization_controls.py.
  • Experiment id: article_text_subset_randomization_controls_v1.
  • Small-n threshold: SMALL_N_THRESHOLD = 30.
  • CORINE primary metric: balanced accuracy from per-class recalls.
  • OSM primary metric: average row-level Jaccard.
  • Headline text sources: content, summary_no_place, and landuse_evidence_summary.

12) Claim wording after this experiment

Use for CORINE: for CORINE, geolocated Wikipedia text is most predictive when both article relevance and spatial label reliability are credible, and this survives controls for subset size and target class composition.

Use for OSM: OSM gains are text-linked for some Qwen content subsets, but target composition and small sample sizes explain more of the apparent improvement than they do for CORINE.

Do not describe Experiment 013 as training or evaluation of a new model. It is a randomization-control analysis over already frozen prediction tables.
