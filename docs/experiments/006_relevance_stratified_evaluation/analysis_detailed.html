Experiment 006: Relevance-Stratified Evaluation

Detailed implementation and result audit for using land-use evidence metadata as a relevance filter over frozen Qwen and Gemma predictions.

1) Executive summary

No LLM rerun

Experiment 006 reuses frozen predictions from the Qwen and Gemma shuffled-control experiments, joins them to land-use evidence metadata, and recomputes metrics by relevance, evidence type, and spatial-confidence subsets. It asks whether the evidence extractor is useful as metadata, even though experiment 005 showed the short land-use summary is too lossy as a replacement classifier input.

4) Metadata used for filtering

Experiment 006 does not introduce a new text representation. It reuses the land-use evidence metadata introduced in experiment 005 and treats it as a filter over already-frozen predictions.

FieldHow it is used here
landcover_relevanceBuilds relevance subsets such as relevance_medium_high.
evidence_typesBuilds evidence-type subsets such as forest, agriculture, water, and relief/geology.
evidence_sentences_countBuilds count-threshold subsets such as at least one or two evidence sentences.
uncertaintyBuilds low- and low/medium-uncertainty quality subsets.
point_label_share_250mCombines evidence relevance with the spatial-confidence filter from experiment 002.

A concrete land-use summary example is shown in the experiment 005 report, where that text source is introduced.

11) Implementation notes

  • Evaluator: evaluate_relevance_stratified_predictions.py.
  • Evidence metadata loader: evidence_metadata_loading.py.
  • Prediction loader: prediction_loading.py.
  • Relevance subsets include individual relevance levels, medium/high, sentence-count thresholds, uncertainty levels, spatial confidence, and spatial + relevance combinations.
  • Evidence type subsets are built for forest, agriculture, vineyard, pasture, water, wetland, shrubland, bare ground, urban/artificial, relief/geology, and habitat/ecology.

12) Interpretation and next step

  • Experiment 005 remains true: the short land-use summary is too lossy as a direct replacement text.
  • Experiment 006 adds the useful role: land-cover relevance metadata is a strong filter for article quality.
  • Spatial reliability and textual relevance compound: the best CORINE rows require both reliable point labels and evidence-rich articles.
  • Less lossy evidence representations are promising: highlights and retrieved windows preserve more raw context than summaries while still focusing the classifier on land-cover cues.

The next high-impact direction is to test evidence cards, highlighted content, or retrieved evidence windows as classifier inputs, using relevance and spatial confidence as analysis filters rather than throwing away raw context.
